# Design Partner Program

## Goal

Use design partners to validate a narrow workflow, not to collect random feature requests.

For ContextGraph, the best current design partners are teams building:

- multi-agent support workflows
- market and research analyst systems
- internal agent platforms that need governed memory sharing

## What We Offer

- direct access to the maintainers
- help with deployment and workflow fit
- fast iteration on real blockers
- early influence on the roadmap

## What We Ask In Return

- a real workflow, not a toy demo
- weekly feedback during the pilot
- permission to learn from usage patterns
- clear success/failure criteria up front

## Best-Fit Customer Profile

Look for teams that already have:

- 2 or more cooperating agents or agent-like workflows
- real memory reuse problems
- need for provenance or review
- sensitivity around access control or cross-team sharing

Avoid for now:

- teams that only want a general chatbot
- teams expecting a full hosted runtime platform
- teams requiring enterprise IAM or compliance export on day one

## Pilot Promise

Keep the promise narrow:

`We help your agents share memory with provenance, review, trust, and controlled visibility.`

Do not promise:

- universal agent infrastructure
- one-click runtime hosting
- finished enterprise control-plane features

## 30-Day Pilot Structure

### Week 1

- choose one workflow
- deploy a pilot environment
- connect the first 2-3 agents
- validate store, follow, recall, and operator visibility

### Week 2

- add discovery profile and cross-team sharing if relevant
- measure what gets stored and recalled
- identify retrieval and governance friction

### Week 3

- tune policy and memory behavior
- review trust/review/governance pain points
- decide whether the workflow is becoming sticky

### Week 4

- assess whether the team would keep using it
- document blockers
- convert to longer design partnership or stop

## Success Metrics

Good pilot signals:

- repeated weekly use by a real team
- memories stored and later reused in real tasks
- follow/discovery used intentionally, not ignored
- review/trust surfaces actually consulted by operators

Bad pilot signals:

- only one person touches it
- memories are stored but never recalled
- team really wanted a runtime/chat platform instead
- product scope has to broaden dramatically just to stay relevant
